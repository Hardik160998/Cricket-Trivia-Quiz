# India Cricket Quiz: A Journey Through the Heart of a Cricket-Crazy Nation

In India, cricket is more than just a sport; it is a religion that unites over a billion people. The journey of the Indian National Cricket Team, from being the underdogs in the mid-20th century to becoming a global superpower, is a story of grit, passion, and unparalleled talent. Every victory and every heartbreak is felt by every Indian household.

### From Test Status to Global Dominance
India gained Test status in 1932, playing their first match against England at Lord's. While the early years were a struggle, the 1970s saw the emergence of the "Spin Quartet" and the legendary Sunil Gavaskar, who became the first player to score 10,000 Test runs. The 1983 World Cup win was the catalyst that turned India into a cricket-obsessed nation, leading to the 2007 T20 World Cup and 2011 ODI World Cup victories.

### Icons Who Defined Eras
The names Sachin Tendulkar, MS Dhoni, and Virat Kohli are etched in gold. Tendulkar, the "God of Cricket," holds almost every batting record imaginable. MS Dhoni, the "Captain Cool," led India to three ICC trophies, while Virat Kohli has taken the team's fitness and aggression to new heights, dominating across all three formats. Other legends like Anil Kumble, Rahul Dravid, and Sourav Ganguly have all played pivotal roles in building India's cricket legacy.

### Iconic Grounds and Memories
From the electric atmosphere of the Eden Gardens to the historic Wankhede Stadium where Dhoni hit the winning six in 2011, India's cricket grounds are cathedrals of the sport. Whether it's the 2001 Kolkata Test victory or the 2021 Gabba heist, India has a knack for creating magical moments.
