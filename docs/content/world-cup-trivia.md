# World Cup Trivia: Reliving Cricket's Greatest Spectacle

The ICC Cricket World Cup is the pinnacle of international cricket, where nations battle for the ultimate glory. Since the first tournament in 1975 held in England, the World Cup has provided some of the most dramatic and unforgettable moments in sporting history. Every four years, the world stops to witness the finest cricketers compete for the prestigious trophy.

### The Early Dominance of the West Indies
The first two editions of the World Cup (1975 and 1979) were dominated by the West Indies. Led by Clive Lloyd and featuring legends like Viv Richards and the "Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse" (their fearsome pace quartet), the Caribbean side was practically invincible. Their style of play brought a new level of aggression and swagger to the game.

### 1983: The Turning Point for India
The 1983 World Cup remains the most significant moment in Indian cricket history. Kapil Dev's "Devils" defeated the mighty West Indies in a low-scoring final at Lord's, changing the course of the sport in the subcontinent forever. This victory inspired a generation of cricketers, including Sachin Tendulkar, and shifted the financial and emotional center of cricket to India.

### Australia's Gold Era and Modern Giants
Following India's win, the late 90s and early 2000s belonged to Australia. Under Steve Waugh and later Ricky Ponting, the Aussies won three consecutive titles (1999, 2003, and 2007), a feat that remains unmatched. In more recent times, the 2019 final between England and New Zealand—decided on boundary count after a Super Over—went down as perhaps the greatest game ever played.
